Bilirubin is a yellow pigment that is produced when red blood cells break down.
Total bilirubin is the sum of both indirect (unconjugated) and direct (conjugated) bilirubin in the blood.
Elevated levels can indicate liver or gallbladder disease.

  

Direct or conjugated bilirubin is a water-soluble form of bilirubin that is produced in the liver by
conjugation with glucuronic acid.
It is then excreted into the bile and ultimately eliminated from the body in feces.
Elevated levels of direct bilirubin can indicate liver or bile duct disease.

  

Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) is an enzyme found in many tissues, including the liver, bones, and intestines.
Elevated levels of ALP can indicate liver or bone disease.

  

Alanine aminotransferase (ALT) is an enzyme found primarily in the liver.
Elevated levels of ALT can indicate liver damage or disease.

  

Total proteins are the sum of all proteins in the blood, including albumin and globulins.
Low levels can indicate liver or kidney disease.

  

Albumin is a protein produced by the liver that helps regulate fluid balance in the body.
Low levels can indicate liver disease or malnutrition.

  

The albumin and globulin ratio is the ratio of albumin to globulins in the blood.
Abnormal ratios can indicate liver disease, kidney disease, or other health  conditions.
