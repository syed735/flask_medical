Women who have had gestational diabetes during pregnancy are at
an increased risk of developing type 2 diabetes later in life.
Additionally, women who have had multiple pregnancies may
also have an increased risk of developing diabetes.

  

Elevated blood glucose levels can be a sign of diabetes, and managing
blood glucose levels is a critical component of diabetes management.
High blood glucose levels can lead to a variety of health problems,
including nerve damage, eye damage, kidney damage,
and cardiovascular disease.

  

High blood pressure is a common comorbidity in individuals with diabetes,
and managing blood pressure is an important aspect of diabetes management.
High blood pressure can increase the risk of cardiovascular disease,
kidney damage,
and other health problems.

  

The thickness of the skin can impact the accuracy of glucose monitoring devices,
as some devices may not be able to penetrate the
skin of individuals with thicker skin.

  

Insulin is a hormone that is critical for the regulation of blood glucose levels.
In individuals with diabetes, the body either does not produce enough insulin or does not use insulin effectively.
Managing insulin levels is an important aspect of diabetes management.

  

Body mass index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight.
Individuals with higher BMIs are at an increased risk of developing diabetes,
as excess body fat can make it more difficult for the body to use insulin effectively.

  

The diabetes pedigree function is a measure of the genetic risk of developing diabetes.
Individuals with a family history of diabetes may be at an increased risk of developing the disease.

  

Age is a risk factor for diabetes, as the risk of developing diabetes increases with age.
Additionally, older individuals may have more difficulty managing their diabetes
due to other health problems or complications.
